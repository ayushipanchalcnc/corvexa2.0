We Were Losing $2M in Pipeline Every Quarter And Our Website Was The Problem

Sarah Chen, Head of Marketing — CyberShield B2B

March 2026 5 min read

If you're reading this, you've probably stared at your website analytics wondering the same thing I was: why are sophisticated, high-intent buyers landing on our product pages — and then just… leaving?

I manage marketing for a B2B endpoint detection and response (EDR) platform. Our buyers are CISOs, IT security directors, and heads of cloud security at companies with 500 to 5,000 employees. These are people who do three to six months of silent research before they speak to anyone.

They were on our site. We just couldn't reach them.

What We Had Already Tried

Generic contact forms

Our ICP never fills them — they feel like commitment before trust is established

Live chat (Intercom)

Just answered support questions, never qualified security intent

Gated content downloads

Email captured, but zero context on what they actually needed

Cold outreach to anonymous visitors

3% reply rate on a good month

Demo request page

Sitting there waiting while buyers chose competitors who engaged them first

The Hidden Truth

Here is the reality of how enterprise security buyers behave on a vendor website.

They land on your threat detection page. They read everything. They check your compliance certifications. They compare your architecture to competitors they already have open in other tabs. They spend 12 minutes doing all of this and then they leave.

They are not ready to book a demo. They are not ready to give you their email. They are absolutely in research mode, and that is exactly when your first impression matters most.

The problem was not our product. The problem was we had no way to start a conversation at that exact moment without asking for a commitment they were not ready to make.

How Corvexa Changed Our Approach

A colleague showed me Corvexa after a competitor of ours appeared to be qualifying leads through their website chat in a way that felt completely different from a standard chatbot. Not a scripted FAQ bot. An actual dialogue that moved the buyer forward.

What made Corvexa different was the intent layer. When a visitor typed something like "how does your threat detection work?" — Corvexa did not just answer the question. It detected that this was a product intent signal, then asked a question designed to pull them into a conversation.

"We recently published a case study with a financial services firm that cut mean time to detect by 70%. Want me to send it across to you?"

The visitor says yes. The lead capture loop begins. Name, work email (validated no personal accounts), company, and team size all collected conversationally. The lead lands in HubSpot tagged with the specific product interest and buyer context before a single rep touches it.

Results

Week 1 :

First qualified CISO-level lead captured from a Fortune 1000 financial firm. That visitor had been on our site 4 times previously and never engaged.

Week 3 :

Our SDR team noticed a different quality in Corvexa-sourced leads. They already knew which product they were interested in. Discovery calls shortened from 45 minutes to 20.

Month 2 :

31 qualified leads captured that month vs. 9 from our previous chatbot. Close rate on Corvexa leads was 28% vs. 11% on form submissions.

Month 3 :

Two enterprise deals closed — both started as Corvexa conversations. Total pipeline added in 90 days: $1.4M.

Honest Assessment: Pros & Cons

What Works Well

  1. Intent detection is the real product it separates research visitors from buying visitors with remarkable accuracy
  2. Company email validation means every lead in HubSpot is a real business contact no Gmail noise
  3. The lead capture loop feels like a conversation, not a form security buyers actually complete it
  4. Memory feature means returning visitors are recognised no repetitive questioning that kills trust
  5. HubSpot sync is instantaneous SDRs get notified within minutes of a qualified lead
  6. Works 24/7 across global time zones our APAC visitors were completely untouched before

Limitations to Know

  1. Setup requires thought — you need to define your intent keywords and selling points carefully before launch
  2. Not suitable for support queries — Corvexa is a sales tool, not a helpdesk replacement
  3. Company email enforcement will miss sole traders and very small firms (deliberate, but worth noting)
  4. Requires a live HubSpot integration to get full value from the CRM sync features
